About Me

I'm Megan Millet — a Ph.D. student in Applied Mathematics at the Illinois Institute of Technology (GPA 4.0), with a B.A. in Mathematics from McGill University and a study abroad semester at EPFL in Lausanne, Switzerland.

My work spans the boundary between pure mathematics and real-world data problems. I'm drawn to graph theory, Markov chains, and probabilistic modeling — and I love applying these rigorously to large-scale datasets.

Outside research, I work as a Consumer Insights Data Analyst at Takasago International, using NLP and LLM-powered tools to extract market intelligence from unstructured data.

I'm committed to mentorship and accessibility in STEM — serving as a teaching assistant, Scientista mentor, and official disability notetaker throughout my academic career.
